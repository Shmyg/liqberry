Global burden

Diabetes is one of the oldest known diseases; its first description can be traced back to 1500 BCE. Today, it is estimated that 382 million people have diabetes and in 2012-2013 diabetes resulted in up to 5 million deaths per year, making it the 8th leading cause of death. According to the forecast, in a single generation only this figure will reach 592 million. Economical and social consequences of this disease are hard to imagine. It is well-known that there is no cure for diabetes. The only preventive known measures are physical exercise, normal body weight and healthy diet.
